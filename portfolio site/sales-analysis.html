Sales Performance & Revenue Decomposition Analysis

Excel Power Query Revenue Analysis EDA

Business Problem

Revenue fluctuations can obscure the underlying health of a retail business when the contributing factors are not clearly understood. Without distinguishing whether changes are driven by customer demand, pricing dynamics, or product performance, decision makers may struggle to implement effective growth strategies.

This analysis was conducted to uncover the drivers of revenue performance using transactional data from a UK-based online retail business over a twelve-month period from December 2010 to November 2011. Incomplete data for December 2011 was excluded to ensure accurate month-over-month comparison.

Key Metrics

  • Total Revenue: £10.2M generated across the analysis period.
  • Total Orders: 19,800 transactions processed.
  • Units Sold: 5.6 million items across all product lines.
  • Average Order Value: £518.28 per order.
  • Customer Base: 4,334 unique customers identified.

Key Findings

  • Sales performance was driven by seasonal demand, with revenue increasing toward the end of the period and peaking in November 2011, indicating concentration of purchasing activity during peak periods.
  • Revenue is driven by high-value products rather than volume alone, with a small number of premium-priced items contributing more to total revenue compared to high-quantity items.
  • Revenue is heavily concentrated in the domestic market, with the United Kingdom contributing the majority of total revenue compared to international markets.
  • Returning customers contributed more to overall sales activity, with repeat purchasing increasing across the period while new customer acquisition declined after the initial peak.
  • Revenue changes were primarily driven by sales volume rather than pricing, with transaction activity having a greater influence on performance across the period.

Recommendations

  • Capture Peak Demand: Align merchandising, marketing, and inventory strategies ahead of peak demand periods to maximize transaction activity.
  • Elevate Premium Products: Increase visibility of high-value products and ensure sufficient stock availability to support their contribution to revenue.
  • Expand Internationally: Strengthen presence in high-performing international markets to reduce reliance on the domestic market.
  • Strengthen Retention and Acquisition: Support repeat purchasing through retention initiatives while sustaining customer acquisition efforts.
  • Drive Off-Peak Transaction Activity: Stimulate purchasing activity during low-demand periods through initiatives that increase volume without relying on price adjustments.

1. Problem Statement

Revenue fluctuations can obscure the true performance of a retail business when the underlying drivers are not clearly understood. Without distinguishing whether changes are driven by customer demand, product value, pricing dynamics, or geographic contribution, decision-making becomes reactive rather than strategic.

This analysis focuses on identifying the key drivers of revenue performance and understanding how product performance, customer purchasing patterns, and geographic contribution collectively influence overall sales outcomes.

2. Objectives

The analysis was conducted to:

  • Examine revenue performance and trends over time
  • Identify the key drivers influencing revenue changes
  • Determine the products contributing most to total revenue
  • Evaluate geographic contribution across markets
  • Analyze customer acquisition, retention, and purchase frequency patterns

3. Dataset Overview

The analysis is based on the UCI Online Retail dataset, which contains transactional records from a UK-based online retailer spanning December 2010 to December 2011.

The dataset includes over 500,000 transactions, with key fields such as invoice date, product description, quantity, unit price, customer ID, and country. These variables support analysis of revenue trends, product performance, customer purchasing behavior, and geographic sales distribution.

4. Tools & Data Considerations

The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel for data cleaning, transformation, and visualization, with Power Query applied to standardize and structure the transactional data.

Data Considerations & Limitations

  • Cancelled orders and returns appear as negative quantities and were excluded to ensure revenue reflects completed sales
  • Customer-level analysis is based on records with valid Customer IDs
  • The dataset covers a one-year period, limiting long-term trend evaluation
  • December 2011 data was excluded due to incompleteness, ensuring consistent month-over-month comparison

5. Data Preparation & Feature Engineering

The dataset was cleaned, standardized, and enhanced to ensure consistency and analytical usability.

Data Cleaning & Standardization

  • Removed duplicate and incomplete records
  • Standardized date formats and text fields
  • Cleaned and normalized product descriptions
  • Ensured numeric fields were correctly formatted

Transaction Classification

  • Identified and excluded non-sales transactions such as cancellations, returns, and adjustments
  • Ensured analysis reflects only completed sales activity

Calculated Columns

  • Revenue: Quantity × Unit Price
  • Time Features: InvoiceDateOnly, Year-Month, Numeric YearMonth for trend analysis
  • Customer Segmentation: First Purchase Date and Customer Type (New vs Returning)
  • Customer Filtering: CustomerID flag for valid customer records
  • Geographic Grouping: Market Group and country classification
  • Product Standardization: Clean Description for consistent product naming

6. Exploratory Data Analysis

Key Metrics

  • Total Revenue: £10.2M
  • Total Orders: 19.8K
  • Total Units Sold: 5.6M
  • Average Order Value: £518.28
  • Total Customers: 4,334

Monthly Revenue Trend

Revenue started at £775.7K in December 2010, declined to approximately £689.8K in January 2011, and remained relatively moderate through mid-year. From September onward, revenue increased significantly, reaching a peak of £1.45M in November 2011. This pattern indicates an early-year dip followed by gradual recovery and strong late-year demand.

Product Performance

Product performance varied significantly between revenue contribution and quantity sold. REGENCY CAKESTAND 3 TIER generated the highest revenue at approximately £168.1K from just over 13,000 units, while MEDIUM CERAMIC TOP STORAGE JAR recorded the highest quantity sold at over 77,000 units but generated comparatively lower revenue. This highlights a distinction between high-volume products and high-value products in driving total revenue.

Geographic Revenue Contribution

Revenue was heavily concentrated in the United Kingdom, which contributed approximately £8.73M of total revenue, compared to £1.52M from international markets. Within international markets, the Netherlands and Ireland were the highest contributors, generating approximately £272.1K and £263.7K respectively. This indicates a strong reliance on the domestic market for revenue generation.

Customer Behavior (New vs Returning & Purchase Frequency)

New customers peaked at 884 in December 2010 before declining in subsequent months, while returning customers increased steadily, reaching 1,337 in November 2011. Purchase frequency remained relatively stable between 1.4 and 1.6 purchases per customer, increasing slightly to 1.7 in November 2011, indicating stronger repeat purchasing during peak demand periods.

Revenue Decomposition: Volume vs Price Effect

Revenue changes were influenced by both volume and price effects, with volume showing greater variability. Volume effect contributed to a decrease of approximately £168.6K in January 2011 and a significant increase of approximately £254.8K in September 2011. Price effects were generally smaller, with a moderate increase of approximately £122K in November 2011. This indicates that transaction volume had a stronger influence on revenue changes than pricing.

Revenue Change Breakdown: Waterfall Analysis

Between October and November 2011, revenue increased from approximately £1.1M to £1.4M. This increase was primarily driven by volume effect, which contributed approximately £226.7K, while price effect contributed a smaller increase of approximately £93.9K. This reinforces the role of increased purchasing activity as the main driver of peak-period performance.

7. Insights

  • Sales performance is highly seasonal, with demand building toward the end of the year and peaking in November, indicating dependence on peak purchasing periods
  • Revenue is influenced by both product value and sales volume, with premium-priced products contributing disproportionately to total revenue
  • Revenue generation is highly concentrated in the domestic market, indicating reliance on the United Kingdom relative to international markets
  • Returning customers play a central role in sustaining sales performance, with repeat purchasing driving growth toward the peak period
  • Revenue fluctuations are driven primarily by transaction volume rather than pricing, with increased purchasing activity being the dominant factor

8. Recommendations

  • Align merchandising, marketing, and inventory planning with peak demand periods to maximize seasonal revenue
  • Increase visibility and availability of high-value products to strengthen their contribution to total sales
  • Expand presence in high-performing international markets to reduce reliance on the domestic market
  • Strengthen customer retention while sustaining acquisition to support repeat purchasing behavior
  • Drive purchasing activity during off-peak periods through initiatives that increase transaction volume without relying on price adjustments
